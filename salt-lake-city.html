Salt Lake City Water Filtration

Local water purification services for Salt Lake City homes and businesses. PureFlow systems you own, installed and maintained by technicians in your neighborhood.

Salt Lake City's municipal water comes primarily from mountain snowmelt collected in Big Cottonwood, Little Cottonwood, Parleys, and City Creek canyons. While the treatment process meets federal standards, the water picks up dissolved minerals as it travels through aging infrastructure to your tap. TDS readings in the Salt Lake Valley commonly range from 250 to 450 ppm, depending on your neighborhood and the time of year.

Hard water is a particular issue across Salt Lake County. The calcium and magnesium content causes white scale buildup on fixtures, reduces water heater efficiency, and leaves residue on dishes and glass shower doors. Over time, it can shorten the lifespan of appliances and increase energy bills.

PureFlow serves Salt Lake City, Murray, Holladay, Millcreek, South Salt Lake, and surrounding communities with residential reverse osmosis systems, whole house filtration, water softeners, and commercial hydration stations. Every system is installed by a local technician and backed by ongoing maintenance.
